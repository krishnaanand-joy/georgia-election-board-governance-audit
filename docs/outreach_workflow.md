# Outreach Workflow

This project required direct outreach to county offices across Georgia.

## Outreach channels

The research workflow used:

- county website searches
- direct phone calls
- follow-up emails
- referrals between county offices
- document requests
- source-link collection

## Outreach challenges

County responses varied significantly.

Some offices were responsive and helpful. Others were difficult to reach, did not respond to repeated emails, redirected requests, or resisted providing clear documentation.

The project required persistence, organization, and professional communication under difficult conditions.

## Tracking outreach

Each county record needed a clear outreach status, such as:

- Not started
- Website reviewed
- Called
- Emailed
- Follow-up sent
- Responded
- No response
- Referred to another office
- Escalate for review

## Why outreach tracking mattered

For a statewide research project, outreach status is part of the evidence trail.

A missing document can mean several different things:

- the document does not exist,
- the document exists but is not online,
- the wrong office was contacted,
- the county has not responded yet,
- the document is titled differently than expected,
- or additional legal research is required.

Tracking outreach helped avoid overstating findings and made the project more useful for staff review.
