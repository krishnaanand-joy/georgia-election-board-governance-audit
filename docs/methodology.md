# Methodology

This methodology summarizes a sanitized public version of the election-board governance research workflow.

## Research goal

The goal was to support review of Georgia county election-board governance by identifying whether each county had clear documentation for its board structure, enabling legislation, and election-administration authority.

The project was designed to help legal and policy staff distinguish between:

- documentation that was located and review-ready,
- documentation that was incomplete or unclear,
- counties requiring follow-up outreach,
- and counties requiring additional legal or policy review.

## Scope

The research workflow covered all 159 counties in Georgia.

The internal work involved direct outreach, public-source review, structured data entry, and repeated follow-up.

This public repository does not publish county-specific findings or internal work product.

## Research steps

The project followed a county-by-county process:

1. Start with the full Georgia county list.
2. Search county websites and public document repositories.
3. Look for board of elections pages, local acts, enabling legislation, appointment rules, and governance descriptions.
4. Contact county offices by phone when documentation was unclear or unavailable.
5. Send follow-up emails requesting documentation or clarification.
6. Track whether the county responded.
7. Record whether documentation was located, unclear, not found, or pending.
8. Assign confidence levels.
9. Flag unresolved items for staff review.

## Data-quality approach

The workflow separated confirmed information from unresolved questions.

A county was not treated as a legal violation simply because documentation was hard to find. Instead, records were categorized for further review based on documentation availability, outreach status, source quality, and confidence level.

## Review language

This project uses careful review language:

- "documentation gap" instead of "violation"
- "flag for staff review" instead of "noncompliant"
- "unclear source status" instead of "no legal authority"
- "potential governance concern" instead of a legal conclusion

Legal conclusions were left to qualified staff.
